# User Research

Managing multiple Kubernetes clusters is complex. We're conducting user research
to understand the real challenges engineers face and build better solutions.

## What We've Done So Far

We started with a user survey to identify key pain points. Now we're moving to
in-depth interviews to dig deeper into the problems you encounter daily.

### Introduction and Consent for User Interviews


> Thank you for taking the time to participate in this semi-structured
> interview!
> 
> The Kubernetes SIG Multicluster UX Working Group is conducting a user
> interview to gather insights from users, operators, and developers on how
> multicluster Kubernetes environments are being used in practice. Your feedback
> will help us understand current usage patterns, pain points, and areas for
> improvement.
> 
> Whether you're just starting with multicluster setups or managing complex
> environments, your input is incredibly valuable and will help shape future
> design directions, feature priorities, and community resources. There are no
> right or wrong answers, I’m just looking to understand your reality.
>
> This is an official user research of SIG Multicluster of Kubernetes.The user
> interview will take approximately 45-60 minutes to complete. 
> 
> With your permission, I’ll record this session for research purposes only. 
>
> All research data will be fully anonymized. Your name, company, email, and all
> direct verbatim responses and recordings will never be shared in our findings.
> We will only report aggregated insights and anonymous, generic quotes to
> protect your privacy. These aggregated key insights and/or anonymous, generic
> quotes will be shared publicly on SIG Multicluster meetings, Kubecon or
> related convention presentations, and/or research papers on the Kubernetes or
> SIG-Multicluster website. 
> 
> Raw research data will be shared only with the User Research team and SIG
> Multicluster SIG-Chairs (who are responsible for running operations and
> processes governing the SIG) for the purposes of the research study only. 
> 
> Participation is completely voluntary. Is that okay?
